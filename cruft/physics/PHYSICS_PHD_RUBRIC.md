# Physics PhD Curriculum Rubric
## Specialization: Thermodynamics & Solid State Matter

This rubric outlines the typical progression and core topics for a Physics PhD with specialization in Thermodynamics and Solid State Matter.

---

## Core Foundation Courses (Year 1)

### 1. Classical Mechanics
- Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formalism
- Oscillations and waves
- Central forces and orbital mechanics
- Rigid body dynamics
- Continuum mechanics basics

### 2. Electromagnetism
- Electromagnetic theory and Maxwell equations
- Electrostatics and magnetostatics
- Wave propagation and radiation
- Special relativity foundations
- Plasma physics introduction

### 3. Quantum Mechanics (Part I)
- Wave-particle duality
- Schr√∂dinger equation
- Operators and observables
- Perturbation theory
- Spin and angular momentum

### 4. Quantum Mechanics (Part II)
- Identical particles and quantum statistics
- Approximation methods
- Scattering theory
- Path integrals
- Quantum field theory introduction

---

## Advanced Core Courses (Year 1-2)

### 5. Thermodynamics & Statistical Mechanics
- Laws of thermodynamics
- Entropy and free energy
- Phase transitions
- Critical phenomena
- Ensemble theory (microcanonical, canonical, grand canonical)
- Partition functions
- Transport phenomena

### 6. Solid State Physics Fundamentals
- Crystal structures and lattice dynamics
- X-ray diffraction
- Phonons and vibrational properties
- Band theory of solids
- Semiconductors and insulators
- Metals and electron transport

### 7. Modern Physics Topics
- Atomic physics
- Molecular physics
- Nuclear physics
- Particle physics basics

---

## Specialization Courses (Year 2-3)

### 8. Advanced Thermodynamics
- Thermodynamic potentials and Maxwell relations
- Phase diagrams and equations of state
- Thermodynamic stability
- Surface thermodynamics
- Interfacial phenomena
- Thermal properties of materials

### 9. Condensed Matter Physics I
- Electron-phonon interactions
- Superconductivity theory (BCS theory)
- Superfluidity
- Magnetism fundamentals
- Magnetic ordering in solids

### 10. Condensed Matter Physics II (Solid State Matter)
- Crystal defects and impurities
- Dislocation theory
- Point defects and vacancy phenomena
- Grain boundaries
- Mechanical properties of solids
- Thermal conductivity in solids
- Electron localization

### 11. Materials Characterization & Experimental Methods
- Spectroscopy techniques (XRD, XPS, Raman, IR)
- Electron microscopy
- Scanning probe techniques (AFM, STM)
- Calorimetry and thermal analysis
- Mechanical testing methods
- Surface analysis techniques

### 12. Phase Transitions & Critical Phenomena
- Order-disorder transitions
- Ferromagnetic transitions
- Superconducting transitions
- Structural phase transitions
- Critical exponents and universality
- Renormalization group theory
- Scaling theory

---

## Advanced Specialization (Year 3+)

### 13. Solid State Thermodynamics
- Thermodynamic properties of crystals
- Lattice models (Einstein model, Debye model)
- Specific heat and Debye temperature
- Thermal expansion
- Defect thermodynamics
- Thermodynamics of interfaces
- Multi-phase systems in solids

### 14. Defects & Disorder in Solids
- Point defect thermodynamics
- Defect concentrations and statistics
- Impurity effects
- Disorder and amorphous solids
- Glass transitions
- Plasticity and deformation
- Creep and relaxation phenomena

### 15. Advanced Materials Science
- Alloys and phase equilibria
- Diffusion in solids
- Nucleation and growth
- Microstructure evolution
- Sintering and densification
- Crystal growth techniques
- Thin films and nanostructures

### 16. Computational Methods in Condensed Matter
- Molecular dynamics simulations
- Monte Carlo methods
- Density functional theory (DFT)
- Ab initio methods
- Lattice models and simulations
- Neural networks and machine learning in materials

---

## Research & Specialized Topics (Year 3+)

### 17. Emerging Topics in Thermodynamics
- Non-equilibrium thermodynamics
- Fluctuation theorems
- Stochastic thermodynamics
- Active matter
- Biological thermodynamics

### 18. Emerging Topics in Solid State Matter
- Topological materials
- Graphene and 2D materials
- Quantum dots and nanoparticles
- Multiferroic materials
- Frustrated spin systems
- Quantum phase transitions

### 19. Specialized Experimental Techniques
- High-pressure physics
- Low-temperature physics
- High-resolution microscopy
- Synchrotron radiation techniques
- Neutron scattering
- Time-resolved spectroscopy

### 20. Thesis Research & Seminar Topics
- Literature review in specialization
- Current research frontiers
- Independent research projects
- Journal club presentations
- Departmental seminars
- Conference participation

## Key Competencies by Specialization

### Thermodynamics Track
- Advanced equilibrium and non-equilibrium thermodynamics
- Phase transitions and critical phenomena
- Statistical mechanical foundations
- Materials processing thermodynamics
- Energy conversion systems

### Solid State Matter Track
- Crystal structure and defect physics
- Mechanical and thermal properties
- Microstructure control
- Advanced characterization techniques
- Materials design principles

### Combined Track (Recommended)
- All competencies above
- Thermal and mechanical property correlations
- Defect thermodynamics
- Phase transformation thermodynamics
- Computational materials design
